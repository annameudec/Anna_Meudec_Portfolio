Hobbies

Interior Design

One key area of interest of mine has always been a love for interior design. From a young age, I have always had a creative streak and a strong passion for art, DIY and re-designing rooms in my home. After years of watching design shows on television and creating mood boards for potential spaces, last summer, having saved up a “Creative Fund”, I decided to embark on my first big project. A space that hadn’t been changed since around the age of 13, I chose my childhood bedroom to become my muse for my project. As always, I began by creating a mood board, choosing an aesthetic and colour palette, and creating a mock-up layout for my future space. Once the planning was finalised and new furniture was chosen, the true task of clearing out the whole space in order for painting and furnishing began! While re-designing and doing DIY, I reflected on how “childhood me” would never believe that I was actually getting to work on my own space and make whatever choices I wished, with no one stopping me!

After completing the project, I felt a massive sense of accomplishment, but I was also eagerly scouting out my next space to re-design. Alongside my before and after pictures here, I have also attached links to some of my favourite DIY and Design channels on YouTube. More recently, this summer, I will be moving to a new apartment, which will soon become my biggest project to date! I am so eager for the project ahead that I have already begun creating a mood board, which you can check out below!

Cinema

Another big hobby of mine is watching movies and going to the cinema, something I always got excited about once a month as a child. Despite this, over the years, I found myself watching fewer and fewer movies and instead just scrolling through reels or YouTube videos. However, my love for watching and analysing films was restored with abundant free time at home during the COVID-19 pandemic! This is something that, during my time studying at university, has given me a great sense of comfort whilst being away from home, a reason to get out of the house during busy weeks and also just a time to switch off and focus on something else.

Until moving abroad and living in Spain for a year, I was never a huge fan of watching films in any other language but English. However, with a love for cinema and no other choice, I still went to the cinema every other week. Over the course of the year, I actually now prefer watching films in Spanish, as it immensely improved my listening skills and also my overall understanding of Spanish culture and lifestyle. With this, I switched from writing film critiques in English to writing them in Spanish in order to keep practising my Spanish written skills!
